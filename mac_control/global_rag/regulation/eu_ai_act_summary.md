# EU AI Act — Relevant Provisions for AI Hiring Tools
# Source: Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU Artificial Intelligence Act)
# This is a summary for agent reasoning — not legal advice.

## Classification
Article 6 + Annex III: AI systems used in employment decisions are HIGH RISK.
This includes: CV screening, candidate ranking, performance evaluation, promotion decisions.

## Obligations for High-Risk AI Systems

### Article 9 — Conformity Assessment
Organisations must conduct a conformity assessment before placing a high-risk AI system on the market.
Self-assessment pathway available for most categories.
Minimum timeline: 5-7 months for thorough compliance.

### Article 11 — Technical Documentation
Full technical documentation must be prepared and maintained.
Must describe: purpose, capabilities, limitations, training data, performance metrics.
MUST BE COMPLETED BEFORE Article 9 conformity assessment can begin.

### Article 13 — Transparency and Logging
AI systems must be transparent and provide logging of operations.
Logs must be kept for at least 6 months.
Individual-level decision logging required for employment AI.

### Article 14 — Human Oversight
Effective human oversight mechanisms must be implemented.
Humans must be able to override AI decisions.
Must be documented and operational before launch.

### Article 51 — Registration
High-risk AI systems must be registered in the EU AI database before market placement.

### Article 68 — Pre-Market Consultation
Organisations may request pre-submission consultation meetings with the EU AI Office.
Reduces informal review time by 40-60%. Standard practice for experienced compliance teams.

### Article 71 — Fines
Non-compliance: up to EUR 30,000,000 or 6% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.
Intentional non-compliance (Article 71(4)): may exceed standard maximum.

### Article 73 — Notification Procedures
Specific notification procedures have 30-day timelines.
NOTE: This does NOT apply to conformity assessment informal review, which averages 60-90 days.

## CE Marking
Required for high-risk AI systems before EU market entry.
Issued after successful conformity assessment.
